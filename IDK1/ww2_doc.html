Introduction

World War II, fought from 1939 to 1945, was a global conflict that reshaped the world.

It involved most of the world's nations, forming two major military alliances: the Allies and the Axis.

What you should already know

World War I ended in 1918 with the Treaty of Versailles, which imposed harsh reparations on Germany.

This treaty and the global economic depression of the 1930s set the stage for renewed conflict.

Why it started

The rise of fascist leaders like Hitler, Mussolini, and militaristic Japan fueled aggression.

Germany's invasion of Poland in September 1939 triggered declarations of war by Britain and France.

How it started

Germany used blitzkrieg tactics, rapidly defeating Poland and moving westward.

The early years of the war saw the Axis powers making significant territorial gains.

How it ended

The Allies launched Operation Overlord (D-Day) on June 6, 1944, to liberate Western Europe.

Germany surrendered on May 8, 1945, amd Japan followed on September 2, 1945.

Major events and turning points

1st September 1939: Germany invaded Poland, marking the official start of WWII.

3rd September 1939: Britain and France declared war on Germany in response to the invasion of Poland.

10th May 1940: Germany launched a Blitzkrieg attack on Western Europe, invading Belgium, the Netherlands, and France.

26th May - 4th June 1940: The Dunkirk evacuation (Operation Dynamo) saved over 300,000 Allied soldiers trapped in France.

22nd June 1940: France formally surrendered to Germany.

10th July - 31st October 1940: The Battle of Britain saw the Royal Air Force successfully defend the UK from German air attacks.

22nd June 1941: Germany launched Operation Barbarossa, a massive invasion of the Soviet Union.

7th December 1941: Japan attacked Pearl Harbor, prompting the US to join the war.

4th - 7th June 1942: The Battle of Midway was a crucial naval victory for the US in the Pacific.

23rd October - 11th November 1942: The Second Battle of El Alamein marked a turning point in North Africa in favor of the Allies.

17th July 1942 - 2nd February 1943: The Battle of Stalingrad ended in a decisive Soviet victory, turning the tide on the Eastern Front.

6th June 1944: D-Day (Operation Overlord) saw the Allied invasion of Nazi-occupied France via the beaches of Normandy.

16th December 1944 - 25th January 1945: The Battle of the Bulge was Germany's last major offensive in the West.

30th April 1945: Adolf Hitler committed suicide in Berlin.

7th May 1945: Germany unconditionally surrendered to the Allies, marking VE Day on 8th May 1945.

6th and 9th August 1945: The US dropped atomic bombs on Hiroshima and Nagasaki, leading to Japan's surrender.

2nd September 1945: Japan formally surrendered aboard the USS Missouri, officially ending World War II.

Consequences and legacy

World War II reshaped the world politically, economically, and socially. The United Nations was formed to promote peace, and the United States and the Soviet Union emerged as superpowers, ushering in the Cold War era.
